Bell Inequality Simulation – A Classical Illusion

A Minimal Classical Setup to Challenge Maximal Quantum Claims

This prototype demonstrates how outcome correlations that appear to violate Bell's inequality can be produced using only classical components, deterministic logic, and synchronized timing — without quantum physics or entanglement.

Two independent nodes, each with their own local axis selector, produced measurement outcomes that — when compared — show apparent correlations depending on axis pairings. The illusion of quantum nonlocality is created through timing synchronization and algorithmic logic.

Download Prototype ZIP

No entangled particles. No photons. No spooky action. Just synchronized logic and local axis decisions.

“What if the mystery was never quantum... but our assumptions?”

Educational Note:

This simulation is intended as an open-ended educational experiment for students, educators, and logic circuit designers interested in foundational questions. It does not prove or disprove any physical theory — but it demonstrates that similar patterns to quantum experiments can emerge from purely classical systems.

This project does not reveal or rely on any known or unknown physical theory. It is a technical simulation designed for educational purposes.

Advanced Mode: Waveform Drift Simulation

By enabling waveform drift logic, this simulation introduces a small deterministic variability between signal generation and detector axis rotation. This creates the appearance of probabilistic correlations — mimicking quantum randomness — while remaining fully local and reproducible.

This feature challenges the assumption that randomness and violation of inequalities are exclusive to quantum systems.
